# Cross-Sector AI Governance Capstone

## Unified Responsible AI Operating Model
**Kwame Thomas | Responsible AI & Governance**

---

## Purpose

This capstone synthesizes Responsible AI governance principles across
multiple regulated and public-interest domains, including:

- Residential real estate
- Education systems
- Regulated retail / cannabis

It demonstrates how organizations can move from fragmented AI controls
to a **cohesive, enterprise-level AI governance operating model**.

---

## Core Thesis

AI governance must be:
- **Risk-based**, not one-size-fits-all
- **Context-aware**, not purely technical
- **Operational**, not aspirational

This capstone shows how AI risk, accountability, and oversight can be
consistently applied across sectors while respecting domain-specific
regulatory requirements.

---

## Governance Model Components

### 1. Unified AI Risk Taxonomy
A shared framework for identifying and prioritizing AI risks such as:
- Bias and disparate impact
- Privacy and data misuse
- Opaque or unexplainable decisions
- Model drift and vendor risk

---

### 2. Lifecycle Governance Controls
AI systems are governed across their full lifecycle:
- Design and data sourcing
- Deployment and decision support
- Monitoring and audit
- Retirement and decommissioning

---

- Compliance and enforcement risk (regulated retail)
